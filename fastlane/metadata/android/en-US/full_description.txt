Wird helps you memorize the Quran, the 40 Hadith of an-Nawawi, and daily
duas from Hisnul Muslim — at your own pace, fully offline, with no account
and no server. Your progress stays on your device (export/import lets you
back it up or move it to a new one).

<b>Traditional method, modern tools</b>
Sessions follow the classic hifz structure — Sabaq (today's new lesson),
Sabqi (recent revision), and Manzil (long-term revision) — built on a
spaced-repetition scheduler (SM-2) so review timing adapts to how well you
actually know each portion. New material uses a listen-repeat-fade-recall
flow; review uses hidden-text self-grading (Again/Hard/Good/Easy).

<b>Choose your own plan</b>
Memorize the whole Quran, specific surahs, or specific juz — in normal
mushaf order or the traditional reversed order (juz 30 first). Set your own
daily time budget; the app estimates your completion date and adjusts as
you go.

<b>Content, verified</b>
Quran text (Uthmani script), the Saheeh International translation and
transliteration, the 40 Hadith of an-Nawawi (Arabic, English, and a short
summary for understanding), and Hisnul Muslim's duas and morning/evening
adhkar — every source is documented in-app (Settings → About) for full
transparency.

<b>Stay motivated</b>
Wird tracks daily streaks with freeze tokens for the occasional missed day, an
"ease-back" mode that shrinks your portion for a couple of days after a
longer gap, a weekly-goal ring, achievement badges, and a GitHub-style
activity heatmap.

<b>Prayer-time awareness</b>
Pick your city (no location permission needed) for local Sunni prayer
times — used to show morning/evening adhkar reminders around Fajr and Asr,
and calculated fully offline as a fallback if you're not connected.

<b>Privacy</b>
No account, no analytics, no ads, no network access beyond fetching
recitation audio and (optionally) online prayer-time tables — everything
else works completely offline. Fully open source (GPL-3.0).
